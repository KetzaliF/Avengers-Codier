Natasha Romanoff is the name of Black Widow, one of the most famous characters in Marvel comics and also one of the most popular since it appeared in the UCM (Marvel Cinematic Universe). Now everyone identifies her as what she is, an Avenger who fights on the side of the good guys, however, many do not know that before doing good, Black Widow was a villain created to be the antagonist of Iron Man.

This character first appeared in Tales of Suspense number 52, published in April 1964. Created and devised by Stan Lee (and drawn by Don Heck), Natalia Alianovna Romanova, which is her original and full name, was an agent Secret Russian trained in the Red Room with her "Black Widow" program, where female spies were "created" and trained, trained (and medicated to improve their skills) with the ever aim of serving the USSR.
